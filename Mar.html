Goda Devi

The Divine Incarnation

Andal (8th Century) is the only female among the twelve Alvar saints of South India. Found as a baby under a Tulsi plant in the temple garden of Srivilliputhur by Vishnuchitta (Periyalvar), she grew up breathing the air of devotion.

She is celebrated as 'Chudikudutha Sudarkodi'—the woman who gave the Lord the garland she had first worn herself. Her love was not just devotion; it was a sovereign claim over the Divine.

Sacred Compositions

Tiruppavai

A collection of 30 stanzas sung during the month of Margazhi. It blends Vedic philosophy with poetic simplicity to awaken the soul to seek the grace of Lord Narayana.

Nachiar Tirumozhi

143 verses expressing Andal's intense longing for Lord Ranganatha, including the famous 'Varanam Aayiram', describing her dream wedding with the Lord.
