Meera Bai

"Mere toh Giridhar Gopal, Dusara na koi"

The Rebel Princess

Born in 1498 in the fortress of Kudki, Meera Bai was a Rajput princess who redefined the meaning of love and royalty. While her family sought political alliances, Meera's heart was already wedded to Lord Krishna.

She famously refused to follow the traditional 'Stri-Dharma' of a royal wife, instead spending her days in the company of wandering saints and dancing in the streets of Chittorgarh.

The Eternal Vow

"I have found the wealth of the Divine Name; a treasure that no thief can steal, and that grows more beautiful every day."

Legacy: Her refusal to commit Sati and her choice of Sant Ravidas as her Guru were revolutionary acts against the caste and gender norms of the 16th century.

The Divine Interventions

The Cup of Poison

When her in-laws sent a cup of lethal poison to end her life, Meera offered it to Krishna and drank it. By divine grace, the poison turned into sweet nectar, leaving her unharmed.

The Serpent & The Flower

A basket containing a deadly cobra was sent to her. When Meera opened the basket, the snake had miraculously transformed into a garland of fresh roses.
