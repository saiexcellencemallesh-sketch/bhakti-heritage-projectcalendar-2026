Sant Tukaram Maharaj

The Infinite Wealth of Devotion

"Truth is the only freedom."

The Voice of the Masses

Sant Tukaram Maharaj (17th Century) was the pinnacle of the Bhakti Movement in Maharashtra. A simple householder turned saint, his Abhangs (devotional poems) broke the barriers of caste and class, teaching that God (Vitthala) resides within the heart of the pure.

Quick Facts:
  • ✨ Deity: Lord Vitthala of Pandharpur
  • ✨ Work: Tukaram Gatha (4,500+ Verses)
  • ✨ Philosophy: Selfless Devotion & Internal Purity

Spiritual Insight: Saving the Soul

▶ TOUCH TO WITNESS
"O Panduranga, people call me mad for losing my business. They don't see that you have cleared my debts to the world so I can invest in the eternal."

Context: After losing his family and business in a great famine, Tukaram turned entirely to spiritual life, viewing his worldly "failures" as divine gifts that removed distractions from his path to God.
