The Path of Nadopasana

Saint Tyagaraja (1767–1847) was the crown jewel of Carnatic music. For him, music was a bridge to the Divine. He viewed Nadopasana—worship through sound—as the most direct path to reaching Lord Rama.

Choosing a life of simple devotion over worldly riches, he famously sang "Nidhi Chala Sukhama?" (Does wealth bring happiness?) when offered royal treasures.

"To see the Divine in every raga, to feel Rama in every tala; that is the true purpose of music."
