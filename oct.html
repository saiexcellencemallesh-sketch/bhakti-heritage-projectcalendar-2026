The Complete Story of a Rebel Saint

Akka Mahadevi (1130â€“1160) was a legendary mystic who broke every rule of her time to find God. Born in Udutadi, Karnataka, she was a devotee of Lord Shiva from childhood. She didn't just pray to Him; she fell in love with Him, calling Him Chennamallikarjuna (The Lord White as Jasmine).

Her life took a turn when King Kaushika fell in love with her beauty. Despite her refusals, she was forced into a marriage on three conditions. When the King broke his promise, Akka did something that shocked the 12th century: she renounced the palace, her riches, and even her clothes. She declared that her body belonged to God, not to a man, and walked away covering her form only with her long hair.

She traveled to Kalyana, where she joined the Anubhava Mantapa, a gathering of the greatest saints of the age. There, the great Basavanna and Allama Prabhu recognized her deep wisdom and gave her the title "Akka" (Elder Sister). She eventually traveled to the Kadali forest in Srisailam, where she achieved final union with her Jasmine Lord.

"I have fallen in love with the Beautiful One,
Who knows no death, has no form, no boundaries.
Listen, O Lord white as jasmine,
Chennamallikarjuna is my only husband."
