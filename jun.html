Saint Kabir

Simple Wisdom for Every Soul

The Weaver of Kashi

Saint Kabir (1440–1518) was a simple weaver who lived in the holy city of Kashi. He didn't have fancy degrees or a wealthy background. He spent his days at a loom, weaving cloth, but his mind was busy weaving together the hearts of people from all religions.

In his time, people were fighting over rituals, castes, and scriptures. Kabir laughed at this. He famously said, "The Lord is in me, the Lord is in you, as life is in every seed." He taught Bharat that God doesn't live in big buildings or complicated books, but in the simple truth and kindness we show to each other. His words were so powerful that they challenged scholars and kings alike, making him one of the most loved voices in Indian history.

Can You Finish These Dohas?

Test your knowledge of these timeless verses. Tap an option to reveal the secret meaning.

1. Bura jo dekhan main chala, bura na milya koi;
Jo dil khoja aapna, mujhse _______________ .

(Translation: When I went looking for evil, I found no one evil; when I searched my own heart, I found none...)
A. achha na koi (I found no one good)
B. bura na koi (I found no one worse than me)
C. bada na koi (I found no one great)
Real Talk: Stop Pointing Fingers It's easy to judge everyone else's mistakes online or in person. Kabir says: if you really look at your own heart honestly, you'll realize you have enough of your own stuff to fix. Today's Lesson: Mind your own business and work on yourself first.

2. Dheere dheere re mana, __________________;
Maali seenche sau ghada, ritu aaye phal hoye.

(Translation: Slowly, slowly, O mind! The gardener may water with a hundred pots, but...)
A. sab kuch hoye (everything happens)
B. dheere sab kuch hoye (everything happens slowly in its own time)
C. kuch na hoye apne kaal (nothing happens in its own time)
Real Talk: Patience is a Superpower In our world of instant delivery and instant likes, we want success right now. But nature doesn't work that way. A tree only gives fruit when the season is right, no matter how much you water it. Today's Lesson: Keep doing the work and trust that your "fruit" will come when the time is right.

3. Pothi padh padh jag mua, pandit bhaya na koi;
Dhai aakhar __________, padhe so pandit hoye.

(Translation: Reading big books, the whole world died, yet none became wise; one who reads 2.5 letters of...)
A. prem ka (of love)
B. gyaan ka (of knowledge)
C. satya ka (of truth)
Real Talk: Degrees vs. Decency You can have all the PhDs and fancy degrees in the world, but if you don't know how to treat someone with love and kindness, you haven't learned anything. Today's Lesson: Empathy and love are the highest forms of intelligence.

4. Saai itna deejiye, ja mein kutumb samaaye;
Main bhi bhookha na rahoon, sadhu ___________________.

(Translation: O Lord, give me only so much that my family is sustained; I should not go hungry, nor...)
A. na bhookha jaaye (the guest goes hungry)
B. bhookha na paaye (hunger is not found)
C. pyaasa na rahe (remain thirsty)
Real Talk: Being Happy with "Enough" Everyone wants "more, more, more." Kabir’s prayer is simple: "Give me enough to take care of my family and a little extra to feed anyone who visits my door." Today's Lesson: True wealth is being content with what you need and being generous to others.

5. Kal kare so aaj kar, ___________;
Pal mein pralaya hoyegi, bahuri karega kab.

(Translation: What you plan to do tomorrow, do today; what you plan for today...)
A. aaj kare so ab (what you must do today, do it NOW)
B. aaj kare so parso (do it the day after)
C. aaj kare so baad mein (do it later)
Real Talk: Stop Procrastinating We always say "I'll start my diet on Monday" or "I'll call my parents later." Kabir reminds us that life is unpredictable. If you have something good to do, do it right this second. Today's Lesson: Don't wait for the "perfect moment." Start now.

6. Guru gobind dou khade, kaake laagon paaye;
Balihari guru aapno, _______________.

(Translation: If the Guru and God both stand before me, whose feet should I touch first? I bow to...)
A. jin jeevan ka arth bataaye (who told life's meaning)
B. jin gobind diya milaye (who introduced me to God)
C. jin shishya diya samjhaaye (who taught the student)
Real Talk: Value Your Mentors If God and your teacher both stand in front of you, you should thank your teacher first. Why? Because without the teacher, you wouldn't even know how to find God. Today's Lesson: Be grateful to the people who opened your eyes and guided your path.
